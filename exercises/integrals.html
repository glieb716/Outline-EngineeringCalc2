  2. Trigonometric Substitution

    Evaluate the following integrals. They all require the same trick of making a trigonometric substitution, so only do as many as it takes you to feel good.
    1. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{1+x^2}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{1-x^2}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{1+x^2}}\)
    5. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{x^2-1}}\)
    6. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x\sqrt{x^2-1}}\)
    7. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x\sqrt{1-x^2}}\)
    8. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x\sqrt{1+x^2}}\)
    9. \(\displaystyle\int x^3\sqrt{1-x^2}\mathrm{d}x\)
    10. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{1-x^2}\,\mathrm{d}x\)
    11. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{1+x^2}\,\mathrm{d}x\)
    12. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{x^2-1}\,\mathrm{d}x\)

  3. Trigonometric Substitution

    Evaluate the following integrals. Solutions to these are written up in Paul's Online Math Notes.
    1. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{1-7\omega^2} \,\mathrm{d}\omega\)
    2. \(\displaystyle\int\limits_{-7}^{-5} \frac{2\mathrm{d}y}{y^4\sqrt{y^2-25}}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{9x^2-36x+37}} \)
  4. (Stewart) Evaluate \(\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x^7-x}\). The straightforward approach would be to start with partial fractions, but that would be brutal. Try a substitution.

  5. The \(x \leftrightarrow \frac 1 x\) Trick

    Show that the integral \[ \int\limits_{0}^{\infty} \frac{\ln(x)}{1+x^2} \,\mathrm{d}x \] evaluates to zero by breaking up its domain of integration into two parts based on where the integrand is negative or positive, and using the substitution \(x \leftrightarrow \frac{1}{x}\) on one of those parts. Then use this same trick to show that the value of the following integral doesn't depend at all on the value of the real number \(p\). \[ \int\limits_{0}^{\pi/2} \frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{1+\big(\tan(\theta)\big)^p} \] See this MathSE post for some reading on this trick.
  6. (Stewart) Evaluate \(\displaystyle\int_0^1 \sqrt[3]{1-x^7} - \sqrt[7]{1-x^3} \,\mathrm{d}x\,.\)
  7. (Stewart) If \(n\) is a positive integer, prove that \[\int\limits_0^1 \big(\ln(x)\big)^n \,\mathrm{d}x = (-1)^n n!\,.\]
  8. Evaluate these integrals.
    1. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{1}{x^5+1} \,\mathrm{d}x\)
    2. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{\tan(x)} \,\mathrm{d}x\)
    3. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{1+\mathrm{e}^{2x}}} \)
    4. \(\displaystyle\int \ln\left(\mu + \sqrt{1-\mu}\right)\,\mathrm{d}\mu \)
    5. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}\theta}{1-\sin^4(\theta)} \)
