  2. Trigonometric Substitution

    Evaluate the following integrals. They all require the same trick of making a trigonometric substitution, so only do as many as it takes you to feel good.
    1. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{1+x^2}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{1-x^2}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\)
    4. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{1+x^2}}\)
    5. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{\sqrt{x^2-1}}\)
    6. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x\sqrt{x^2-1}}\)
    7. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x\sqrt{1-x^2}}\)
    8. \(\displaystyle\int \frac{\mathrm{d}x}{x\sqrt{1+x^2}}\)
    9. \(\displaystyle\int x^3\sqrt{1-x^2}\mathrm{d}x\)
    10. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{1-x^2}\,\mathrm{d}x\)
    11. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{1+x^2}\,\mathrm{d}x\)
    12. \(\displaystyle\int \sqrt{x^2-1}\,\mathrm{d}x\)
